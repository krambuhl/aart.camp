## Contract

  the CLI exercising every verb plus representative error paths,
  document the results, mark the phase complete via autosave.
  This is the final deliverable of the project's first (and per

- **Acceptance criteria**:

  **Smoke coverage**:
  1. `bin/draft plan` against a throwaway projects-root creates
     `<date>-<slug>/PLAN.md` and `<date>-<slug>/INTERVIEW.md` and
     emits the expected JSON envelope.
  2. `bin/draft read <slug>` emits the JSON envelope with `path`,
     `content`, `plan: {slug, interview_path}` fields.
  3. `bin/draft read <slug> --pretty` renders PLAN.md content
     directly (no envelope).
  4. `bin/draft revise <slug>` with `--revision-file=` and
     `--rationale=` replaces PLAN.md with revision content,
     appends `- <YYYY-MM-DD> — <rationale>` under a
     `## Revision log` heading, leaves INTERVIEW.md untouched.
  5. Error paths surface structured JSON:
     - `project-not-found` for an unknown slug.
     - `missing-args` when a required flag is absent (e.g.
       `--rationale` on revise).
     - Uncommitted-PLAN-overwrite case (no plan-exists-committed
       error since `--no-commit` was used) succeeds — recovery
       path works as designed.

  **Phase-close ceremony**:
  6. Autosave phase-completed event:
     `Bash("node .claude/scripts/trout/autosave.ts draft-cli --event=phase-completed --detail=1 --phase-update=1:completed")`.
  7. Brief Phase 1 retrospective folded into this checkin's
     `Notes for the PR` section (lessons learned, surprises, what
     to carry forward).

  **Skills smoke (manual / deferred)**:
  - `/draft-plan` and `/draft-revise` skill bodies were authored
    in D6 with explicit step ordering and failure modes. Full

## Notes for the PR

### Phase 1 retrospective

**Shipped**: A complete grill-me-shaped planning substrate.
`bin/draft` with three verbs (plan, revise, read), two skills
(/draft-plan, /draft-revise), shared lib (createSlug,
resolveTroutProject, GitRunner), loop wiring in both
ev-loop-confidence and ev-loop-interactive. The substrate is
functional end-to-end as verified by this smoke.

**What surprised me**:
- The biggest mid-flight scope shift wasn't a planning failure —
  it was the substrate evolving under the project. PR #70 merged
  D1 standalone (revealed per-deliverable PR cadence). Loom's
  `listProjects` formalized the trout/loom marker boundary
  mid-stream (forced the D5 filter broadening). Both are
  precisely the kind of shift `/draft-revise` is designed to
  handle — we got to dogfood the project's own revision flow as
  scope shifts hit PLAN.md.
- The whiteboard step's L-004 session boundary at the runtime
  level (not the disk level) was a real friction point at phase
  start. Required a Claude Code restart to load the
  newly-shipped whiteboard-* agents. Worth a substrate-side
  documentation note.
- Combining D5+D6 and D7+D8 (the user's mid-project request)
  produced cleaner PRs than the original per-deliverable plan.
  Two verbs share enough scaffolding that splitting them costs
  more than it gains; same for two skills.

**What carries forward**:
- The pure/effect split per verb (DraftCliContext with injected
  `gitRunner` and `today`) was the load-bearing testability
  decision. Worth replicating in any future CLI verb work.
- The `signal:`-prefixed note convention for single-signal
  scope-shifts (D7) gives the loop a way to surface drift
  without interrupting. The pattern will probably show up
  elsewhere (any time a loop has heuristic detection with
  too-noisy single-evidence).
- The matched-naming seam between `/draft-plan` (skill) and
  `bin/draft plan` (CLI) was a deliberate decision. Future
  reviewers reading both side-by-side will see the seam
  documented in each.

**Open follow-ups (not in scope for archival)**:
- `LoomError` is a slight name asymmetry for draft. Documented
  in PLAN.md Decisions; substrate-wide rename is a separate
  effort if/when it earns its weight.
- Slug-grammar regexes are duplicated between
  `lib/project.ts` (loom) and `lib/draft-project.ts` (draft)
  because loom doesn't export them. A small substrate cleanup
  could lift them to a shared constants file.
- `/draft-revise` is currently surgical (no grill-me). PLAN.md
  Deferred items name the lift-to-grill-me upgrade if revision
  quality slips. No signal yet.
- Loom-cli is now complete (archived); when `loom project
  scaffold` shipped, draft could in theory delegate the
  directory-creation step. Not a problem today; revisit on
  the next draft-related project.
